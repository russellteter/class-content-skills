# Class Messaging Pillars — Deep Reference

## Core Problem Statement

Generic meeting tools (Zoom, Teams, Webex) give organizations video, chat, and screen share. Those are the basics of communication, not the basics of instruction. Training requires persistent course structures, engagement measurement, breakout oversight, assessment tools, and LMS data flow. When organizations use meeting tools for training, instructors spend time rebuilding sessions, switching between apps, and guessing whether learners are actually engaged.

Class closes that gap by adding a learning layer to the meeting tools organizations already use.

## The Four Solution Pillars

### Pillar 1: Foundation

**Statement:** Everyone learns, anywhere — secure, seamless access with built-in reliability, scalability, and accessibility.

**Supporting messages:**
- Join from any device, any location. Dial-in support, multiple browsers, waiting room.
- Scale sessions to 500 participants while maintaining personalized experience.
- Security from start to finish: ID verification, role-based access, data protection.
- Accessible by design: built-in sign language interpreter role, intuitive interface.

**Key features:** Dial-in support, multi-browser, waiting room, up to 500 participants, virtual backgrounds, security, ID verification, accessibility, sign language interpreter role.

**When to emphasize:** Government content (security), healthcare (compliance), large enterprise (scale).

### Pillar 2: Before Training

**Statement:** Seamless setup, maximum impact — sync your LMS, build templates and assessments, and pre-configure breakout rooms to create training that's repeatable and consistent from day one.

**Supporting messages:**
- Simplify setup by linking your virtual classroom with your LMS. Rostering, attendance, gradebook, and materials sync automatically.
- Build assessments and surveys that shape training around real learning outcomes, not just attendance.
- Use pre-built templates to create sessions in less time. Build once, deliver consistently.
- Configure breakout rooms ahead of time so participants start productive work immediately.

**Key features:** LMS integration (Cornerstone, Docebo, D2L/Brightspace, Canvas, Blackboard), course templates, assessments/quizzes, surveys, engagement analytics baseline, breakout room pre-configuration.

**When to emphasize:** Enterprise academies (consistency across regions), training providers (efficiency at scale), any content about operational efficiency.

### Pillar 3: During Training

**Statement:** Stay in command — manage breakout rooms, monitor progress, and guide learners in real time. Interactive features drive focus, participation, and outcomes.

**Supporting messages:**
- From static to dynamic: whiteboard, screen share, annotations turn passive watching into active learning.
- Assess progress in real time: surveys, polls, quizzes integrated into the session flow.
- Monitor all breakout groups from one view. Drop in, guide, and redirect without disrupting other groups.
- Shape each session to match instructional goals: seating charts, front of room, privacy mode, pinned tabs.
- Track who's present, who's participating, and who needs support in real time.

**Key features:** Whiteboard, screen share/annotations, surveys/polls/quizzes, enhanced breakout rooms, Bird's Eye View with transcription, breakout content launch, webpage sharing, file upload, video playback, MS Office Live Collaboration, seating charts, front of room, privacy mode, instructor podium, attendance tracking, engagement scoring.

**When to emphasize:** Instructor-focused content, cognitive load topics, engagement content, any "during the session" framing.

### Pillar 4: After Training

**Statement:** Keep learning active, insights clear — on-demand content and interactive recordings ensure continuous engagement. Analytics on attendance, engagement, and progress drive better outcomes.

**Supporting messages:**
- Go beyond passive recordings: interactive playback lets learners engage with comments, questions, and embedded polls.
- Learning continues after the session: persistent chat, archived recordings keep content accessible.
- Deeper than attendance: post-session analytics reveal interaction, engagement patterns, and areas for instructor improvement.
- Full session records: downloadable transcripts, chats, session timelines.
- Assessment data (quizzes, surveys, polls) becomes a goldmine for refining instruction.

**Key features:** Interactive Playback, persistent chat, archived recordings, analytics dashboard, session data downloads, recorded transcripts, chat exports, session timeline, assessment/survey reporting.

**When to emphasize:** ROI/measurement content, post-training engagement topics, leadership/executive audience (proving value).

## The Three Advantage Pillars

### Advantage 1: Drive Learner Engagement

**Core message:** Active participation, collaboration, and interaction. Learners are seen, heard, and involved — not silently watching a screen.

**Proof points:**
- Tripod Education Partners study: 5x engagement increase vs. standard Zoom
- Volvo: "a lot more engagement through Class than previous platforms"
- LinkedIn 2025 Workplace Learning Report: 91% of L&D professionals say continuous learning is more critical than ever

### Advantage 2: Scale Access to Quality Instruction

**Core message:** Repeatable, well-crafted virtual learning that ensures every learner — regardless of location — gets the same quality experience.

**Proof points:**
- 10M+ users across 75+ countries
- 1,500+ institutions
- Templates enable consistency across instructors, cohorts, and regions

### Advantage 3: Foster Community & Connection

**Core message:** Distance doesn't limit learning together. Instructors and learners build real relationships through collaborative tools.

**Proof points:**
- 93% of high school students who feel belonging are more likely to stay through graduation
- 91% of workers report greater success when learning alongside others
- 46% of workers would consider quitting if organization doesn't prioritize team trust

## Vertical-Specific Messaging Summary

### Corporate / Enterprise Academies
- **Lead with:** Talent development, retention, business impact, operational efficiency
- **Pain points:** Scattered training across tools, inconsistent delivery, lack of engagement data, manual admin
- **Outcomes:** Reduced onboarding time, improved completion rates, measurable engagement, instructor efficiency
- **Named customers:** Volvo, BCG, Marriott, GoDaddy, Cornerstone

### Government
- **Lead with:** Security, compliance, scale, workforce development
- **Pain points:** Legacy tools, remote workforce adaptation, cybersecurity concerns, recruitment/retention
- **Outcomes:** FedRAMP compliance, agency-wide training scale, compliance verification, data protection
- **Key credential:** FedRAMP-certified

### Training Providers
- **Lead with:** Client ROI proof, competitive differentiation, scaling delivery
- **Pain points:** Proving training value to clients, generic tools limiting differentiation, manual reporting
- **Outcomes:** Engagement analytics for client reports, consistent delivery at scale, client retention through results

### Healthcare
- **Lead with:** Patient safety, competency verification, compliance documentation
- **Pain points:** High-stakes training requiring verification, distributed staff, regulatory requirements
- **Outcomes:** Verified participation, competency tracking, consistent training protocols, compliance documentation
