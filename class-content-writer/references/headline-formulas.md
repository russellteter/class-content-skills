# Headline Formulas — Derived from Published Class Content

## Formula 1: Problem/Solution Reframe
**Pattern:** [Pain Point] + [Solution/Reframe verb]
**Word count:** 9-14 words
**Best for:** How-to posts, operational topics

**Published examples:**
- "How to Make Small Group Work **Actually Work** in Virtual Training"
- "Why Build on Zoom and Teams? A **Smarter Way** to Improve Virtual Training"
- "Reducing Cognitive Load in Virtual Instructor-Led Training"
- "Engaged or Just Logged In? **Rethinking** Success Metrics in Virtual Learning"

**Template:** "How to [Make/Improve/Reduce] [Specific Challenge] in [Virtual Training/VILT Context]"

**Verbs that work:** Make, Improve, Reduce, Rethink, Fix, Strengthen, Simplify

## Formula 2: Outcome-Driven How-To
**Pattern:** How [Feature/Method] Helps [Audience] [Achieve Outcome]
**Word count:** 10-16 words
**Best for:** Feature-benefit posts, vertical-specific content

**Published examples:**
- "How Virtual Instructor-Led Training Helps Companies Become **Career Development Champions**"
- "How Volvo Revved Up Engagement with Virtual Instructor-Led Training"
- "Four Ways Virtual Classrooms Help Healthcare Organizations Train Staff"
- "How Integrated EdTech Saves Time, Cuts Costs, and Boosts Learning Outcomes"

**Template:** "How [Method/Platform] Helps [Specific Audience] [Specific Outcome]"
**Alternative:** "[Number] Ways [Method] Helps [Audience] [Outcome]"

## Formula 3: Assertive Category Claim
**Pattern:** [Bold Statement] + [Implementation Promise]
**Word count:** 8-14 words
**Best for:** Thought leadership, trend analysis, category positioning

**Published examples:**
- "Why **Platform Choice Matters** for Virtual Training"
- "VILT Is the **New Standard** — Here's How to Make Yours Unforgettable"
- "Top 5 Must-See Sessions at ATD 2025: **Learning That Moves the Needle**"

**Template:** "[Category Assertion]: [What You'll Learn/Get]"
**Alternative:** "Why [Thing Everyone Ignores] Matters for [Your Context]"

## Formula 4: Day-in-the-Life / Narrative
**Pattern:** A Day in the Life of [Specific Role]
**Word count:** 9-11 words
**Best for:** Persona-specific content, use-case showcases

**Published examples:**
- "A Day in the Life of **a Training Consultant**: Delivering Better Learning Across Clients"
- "A Day in the Life of **an L&D Director**: How One Leader Delivers Engaging, Scalable Training With Class"

**Template:** "A Day in the Life of [Role]: [Subtitle with Outcome]"

## Formula 5: Customer Story
**Pattern:** How [Company] [Achieved Specific Result]
**Word count:** 8-12 words
**Best for:** Customer stories, case studies

**Published examples:**
- "How Volvo Brings the In-Person Training Experience Online"

**Template:** "How [Company] [Action Verb] [Specific Training Outcome] with [Class/VILT]"

## Headline Rules (Hard Constraints)

1. **9-14 words.** Shorter is better if it's still specific.
2. **Primary keyword included naturally.** Don't force it.
3. **Practical verbs only.** Make, Improve, Reduce, Help, Rethink, Build, Deliver, Strengthen.
4. **No buzzwords.** No "revolutionary," "game-changing," "ultimate," "definitive."
5. **No clickbait.** The headline should accurately describe what the reader will get.
6. **No question-only headlines.** If using a question, pair it with a statement. ("Engaged or Just Logged In? Rethinking Success Metrics")
7. **Specific over generic.** "How to Reduce Cognitive Load in VILT" beats "Improving Virtual Training."
